Elimination

disorders
Enuresis
Elimination

disorders

involve

the

inappropriate

elimination

of

urine

(enuresis)

or
faeces

(encopresis),

that

are

usually

first

diagnosed

in

childhood

or

adolescence.
Enuresis

is

defined

as

repeated

voiding

of

urine

into

the

bed

or

clothes

whether
involuntary

or

intentional,

at

least

twice

per

week

for

at

least

three

consecutive
months

in

a

child

who

is

≥

5

years

of

age,

resulting

in

significant

distress.

It

can
occur

during

daytime

(diurnal)

or

during

nighttime

(nocturnal).

Primary

enuresis

is
bed

wetting

that

occurs

in

a

child

who

has

never

achieved

dryness.

Secondary
enuresis

is

onset

of

enuresis

after

previous

achievement

of

dryness

for

at

least

6
months.
Evaluation

and

management

approaches

for

enuresis
A

careful

history

should

be

obtained

and

a

thorough

physical

examination

done,
urinalysis,

urine

culture.

Urinary

system

ultrasonography

and

urodynamic

studies
should

be

performed

where

indicated

to

exclude

medical/surgical

causes.

If

a
medical

condition

is

suspected

the

patient

should

be

referred

to

the

appropriate
specialist

discipline.

Psychiatrists

are

mainly

involved

in

the

treatment

of

enuresis
where

medical

causes

have

been

excluded.

Behavioural

approaches

are

the

first
line

treatment

for

uncomplicated

enuresis

due

to

non-medical

causes.
Urotherapy

Standard

Nocturnal

positive

enuresis
