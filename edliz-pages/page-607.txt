Chemoradiation
Chemotherapy

is

increasingly

being

administered

concurrently

with

radiotherapy
in

most

tumours.

The

result

of

chemoradiation

in

these

tumours

is

superior

to

that
of

radiotherapy

alone.

The

improved

outcome

outweighs

the

slightly

increased
toxicity

of

the

combined

treatment.

Examples

of

cancers

effectively

treated

by
chemoradiation

include

cervical

cancer,

oesophageal

cancer,

nasopharyngeal
cancer

and

other

head

and

neck

cancers
Maintenance

Systemic

Therapy
In

certain

specific

conditions

may

need

to

on

chemotherapy

or
systemic/targeted

therapy

for

a

defined

period.

Examples

include

tamoxifen

for
ER/PR

positive

breast

cancer

and

rituximab

for

certain

haematologic

malignancies.
Palliative

Systemic

Therapy
Chemotherapy

advanced

for

where

no
alternative

therapy

or

A

use

palliative
chemotherapy.

Example

cancers

palliated

with
chemotherapy

include

advanced

ovarian

cancer,

germ

cell

tumours

of

the

testis,
small

cell

lung

cancer

Immunotherapies

now

cancers

other

therapies

failed.

Excellent

results

realised

some

cancers.
